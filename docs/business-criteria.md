# Business Criteria

## Target Market Principles

Waiting For AI should serve people with common, globally recognizable waiting problems. The product should be useful to someone waiting on a delivery, a refund, a booking response, a repair update, or a message back without requiring local industry knowledge or specialized workflows.

The product should feel understandable across regions, professions, and device habits. Users should not need to learn a methodology. They should only need to recognize that an item is pending on an external party and that they want help deciding when to check again.

## Global Clarity

The product should stay globally understandable because the pain is universal and the workflow is simple. People everywhere wait for replies, decisions, shipments, service outcomes, and confirmations. If the product becomes dependent on region-specific systems, assumptions, or terminology, it will shrink its usefulness and make onboarding harder.

## Simple Purpose

The purpose must remain simple: capture something externally pending, structure it into a waiting item, and help the user revisit it at the right time. A simple purpose improves adoption, supports narrow MVP execution, and keeps product decisions easier to evaluate.

## AI Role

AI should be used for extraction, normalization, and confidence-aware structuring. It should turn messy input such as pasted text, screenshots, or message content into a clear waiting item draft.

AI should not behave like a free-form assistant in the MVP. Open-ended assistant behavior increases ambiguity, changes user expectations, and pulls the product toward chat instead of a dependable waiting workflow.

## Anti-Goals

- Not a general todo app.
- Not a chatbot.
- Not a team collaboration tool.
- Not calendar- or email-sync-heavy software.

## MVP Success Criteria

- Users can add waiting items manually or from unstructured text.
- The app presents a clear distinction between active waiting, upcoming follow-up, inbox/review, and done states.
- AI-related functionality is scoped to structured extraction or visible placeholders for that workflow.
- The product language stays understandable without explaining task management systems.
- Scope remains small enough to ship and learn from within two weeks.
